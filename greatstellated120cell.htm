The Final Stellation of the 120-Cell

On Saturday March 24, 2007, we built a Zome model of the final stellation of the 120-cell at the Lee Honors College at Western Michigan University. Watch this space, as photos will appear soon.

Apparently nobody in the world had ever made a Zome model of this particular mathematical object. In fact, it's likely that nobody had ever made a 3-dimensional model of it, using Zome or any other medium. This construction was recently made possible because Zometool is working on a new set of parts which may allow users to build smaller, lighter models. They're essentially scaling down the lengths of their standard parts by the Golden Ratio.

The construction took about 6 hours. We had plenty of help, about 40 people, but, due to the small size of the model, only a handful of people could work on the model at once.
